# 3-bet pots postflop

3-bet pots are condensed and high-SPR-sensitive. The 3-bettor's range is
linear-polarized (premiums + select bluffs); the caller's range is capped
(usually no AA-QQ, sometimes no AKs).

Standard heuristics in single-raised vs 3-bet pots:

- 3-bettor IP on most boards: c-bet small (~33% pot) at 60-80% frequency.
  Range advantage holds on high-card boards; 3-bettor has more strong-pair
  combos and aces.
- 3-bettor OOP: c-bet small at slightly lower frequency (~50-65%) because
  giving up position costs equity realization.
- Caller in position: defend wide vs small c-bets (folding too much is the
  single biggest leak); attack with check-raises on boards that hit the
  caller's range better (low connected boards in particular).
- Dry, broadway-heavy boards favor the 3-bettor heavily.

SPR is typically ~3-5 in a 3-bet pot, so commitment decisions arrive faster
than in single-raised pots. Stack-off ranges narrow: top pair good kicker is
often a stack-off vs. a single barrel + jam line.

The voice-friendly framing: "3-bet pot, SPR around four. Their range is capped
— no aces or kings — so on Kxx I'm c-betting most hands small. Their best
hand is queens or AK; mine is AA, KK, AK, sets."
