# Big blind defense

BB has a price discount because they already have one big blind invested. This
means BB defends extremely wide vs. small opens, especially in HU where there's
no SB to worry about.

Heads-up against a 2.5x BTN open at 100bb, BB defends roughly:

- ~65-70% of hands (the rest fold)
- 3-bet ~12-15% (linear-polarized mix: premiums + select bluffs like A2-A5s,
  K9o, suited connectors that play well against the 4-bet bluff range)
- Call the rest

The wider the BTN open, the wider BB defends — and the more weighted BB's range
becomes toward marginal made hands and suited connectors that flop well.

At deeper stacks (150bb+), BB starts to flat more and 3-bet less because deep
stacks favor the in-position player who can realize equity more easily. At
shallow stacks (40bb-), BB 3-bets become more polarized (shove or fold style).

The mistake: BB folding "junk" they should be defending. Anything that has 22%
equity vs. opener's range at a 1.5:1 price plus realization is a defend.
