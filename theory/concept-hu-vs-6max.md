# HU vs. 6-max

Heads-up no-limit is structurally different from 6-max because every hand
plays heads-up. Some shifts:

- **Opening ranges**: BTN opens ~84% of hands HU (essentially everything but
  the worst offsuit garbage). 6-max BTN opens ~45-50%.
- **3-bet frequencies**: BB 3-bets ~12-15% HU vs ~8-10% from BB in 6-max.
  Wider opens drag wider 3-bet ranges with them.
- **Postflop range advantages**: more boards favor the in-position player HU
  because the opener's range is wide enough that they hit fewer boards
  strongly. On 765r heads-up, BTN actually has *worse* range advantage than
  in 6-max because their HU range includes more middling cards.
- **Sizings**: HU c-bet sizings can be smaller on average than 6-max because
  the opener's range is wider and less polarized. 25-33% pot is the modal
  HU c-bet; 6-max leans more toward 33-50%.
- **Variance**: HU is dramatically higher variance per hand than 6-max. Same
  hourly winrate, much larger swings.
- **Multi-way considerations**: irrelevant in HU; central in 6-max (a 3-bet
  in 6-max can get called by the original opener AND the cold-caller in the
  blinds, changing the math).

When the buddy is discussing a 6-max spot, it should explicitly note: "Heads-up
ranges are way wider than this. For 6-max from CO, opens are tighter and the
exact frequency depends on opener-vs-caller dynamics."
