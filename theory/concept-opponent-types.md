# Opponent typing and exploits

GTO is the baseline; exploits are the edge. The single biggest mistake mid-stakes
regs make is playing pure GTO against opponents who deviate significantly.

Six common opponent types and the standard exploits against each:

1. **Nit (tight-passive)**: doesn't 3-bet without premiums, folds too much to
   c-bets. Exploit: open wider, c-bet more dry boards, fold to their raises
   without strong reads.

2. **Calling station (tight-passive postflop, loose-passive)**: calls too much
   on flop and turn, only folds rivers. Exploit: value bet thin, never bluff
   the same line twice, triple-barrel only with real equity.

3. **LAG (loose-aggressive)**: 3-bets wide, c-bets wide, barrels wide. Exploit:
   widen calling ranges, slow-play strong hands, set up check-raise traps.

4. **TAG (tight-aggressive reg)**: solid solver-derived ranges. Few exploits
   available; play close to GTO. Edge comes from creative line construction.

5. **Maniac (very loose-aggressive)**: opens 70%+, 3-bets ~25%, doesn't fold.
   Exploit: tighten 3-bet range to value, flat with stronger hands, induce
   bluffs by checking back stronger.

6. **Whale (loose-passive)**: limps, calls down with weak holdings, won't
   fold weak top pair. Exploit: isolate with wide range, value bet relentlessly,
   never bluff multi-street.

The voice-friendly framing: "Sounds like a station. Just value bet — even
top pair second kicker is a three-street bet against him. Skip the bluff."

Update opponent profiles incrementally. One unusual line doesn't redefine a
player; three lines does.
