# Stack depth effects

Stack depth changes everything. Common bands and how they shift strategy:

- **<25bb (short)**: Push-fold dominates. Open-shove ranges, BB defend by
  call-or-jam. Postflop barely exists; most pots are flop check-back or
  flop jam.

- **25-50bb (shallow)**: Still raise-fold preflop but postflop has structure.
  3-bet pots commit; SPR is ~1-2. Suited connectors lose value because you
  can't realize equity. Pocket pairs and broadways gain. Limping from BTN
  becomes mathematically defensible HU.

- **75-150bb (standard)**: The canonical solver range. Most published study
  is in this band. SPR after raise+call ~7-9, after 3-bet pot ~3-5.

- **150-250bb (deep)**: Implied odds and position become massive. BB flats
  more, 3-bets less. Open sizings often shrink to 2-2.2x because the call
  is too profitable for BB to fold many hands. Postflop plays bigger.

- **250bb+ (very deep)**: Specialized. Suited connectors and small pairs
  are gold; pocket aces sets up to be cooler. Set-mining math improves
  but reverse implied odds appear (e.g. KK on Axx becomes terrifying).

The voice-friendly framing: "What's the stack? Hundred big? OK then standard
ranges apply. If you're at thirty big blinds the whole game changes — we'd be
push-fold and limping junk on the button."

The mistake: applying 100bb strategy at 40bb or 200bb. Both deviate from the
solver baseline you've studied.
